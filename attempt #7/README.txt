Population + Natural Selection

Result:
- Saw 38+ rolling (better than before)

Abandoned in favor of pure physics approach.

=== LEARNING MOMENT ===

During this attempt, a realization emerged from conversation:

User: "Evolution is real, but that's AFTER emergence. That started 
happening after there was a spark. Adding selection is putting in 
human rules that we already learned. The only thing it should know 
is physics. You die, and there are factors that change that."

The insight: We were adding layers that biological life took billions 
of years to develop (natural selection, population dynamics, survival 
of the fittest). But those came AFTER the first spark of life.

The experiment should ask: Can the spark itself emerge from pure physics?

Before evolution, there was just:
- Physics (consequences happen)
- Chemistry (things interact)
- Something that learned to persist

We stripped everything back to:
  physics -> N2 -> N1 -> actions -> physics

No evolution. No selection. No designed death signals.
Just physics teaching, and learning happening.

This is the purer experiment.
